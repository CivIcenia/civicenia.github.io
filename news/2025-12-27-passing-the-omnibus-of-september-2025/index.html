Passing the Omnibus of September 2025

26th September 2025

  1. Repeal the Dbuningrad Act

  2. Approve the DGP

  3. Nullify the Benefits Awareness Act

  4. Amend the Building Standards and Processes Act - patch loophole that (debatably) allows for 170+ builds with only SOI approval

Extend no higher than y:170 (exceptions may be made for antenna or other decorative parts). A standard motion by the Senate and SoI approval may bypass this requirement.

to

Extend no higher than y:170 (exceptions may be made for antenna or other decorative** fixtures**). A standard motion by the Senate may bypass this requirement, subject to discretionary review by the Secretary of Interior.

the intended goal of this amendment is to make it so the SOI and the senate must approve a building above the height of 170

By god if you have any finagley bullshit to read it any other way speak it now or forever hold your peace lest I quote this when I spirit of the lawsuit your build to the depths of hell

  1. Amend the Safe Icenia Act - patch loophole that allows a gov official to nuke another gov official using the act

Citizenship and access to socials (Discord, Reddit, etc) and in-game groups may be suspended by a government official following an alleged crime with imminent prosecution if it is believed that their citizenship and or access pose an immediate and genuine threat to informational and or national security. Such a suspension must be as limited in scope and duration as possible, such as being limited to the duration of the trial.

To

Citizenship of non-government citizens may be suspended by a government official following an alleged crime with imminent prosecution if it is believed that their citizenship and or access poses an immediate and genuine threat to informational and or national security. Such a suspension must be announced to the senate, as well as being as limited in scope and duration as possible, such as being limited to the duration of the trial.

Change to exclude gov officials from being citizenship suspended Change to clarify that any suspension must be announced to the Senate
